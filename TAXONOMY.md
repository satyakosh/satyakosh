Rings gate ambition; derivation gates admissibility. This document maps
the kinds of facts the world contains onto what the schema can express and
what the pipeline currently admits.

| # | Category | Ring | Admissibility today |
|---|---|---|---|
| C1 | Defined constants (exact by definition) | 1 | SEAL |
| C2 | Measured constants (with uncertainty) | 1 | SEAL |
| C3 | Mathematical facts | 1 | SEAL |
| C4 | Conditional physical/chemical properties | 1 | SEAL (mandatory conditions enforced) |
| C5 | Taxonomic & compositional facts | 1-2 | partial; entity-valued objects are Ring 2 |
| C6 | Definitional & conventional facts | 2 | RING-2 PENDING (external-standard adoption) |
| C7 | Historical events | 2 | RING-2 PENDING (documentary evidence rules) |
| C8 | Jurisdictional facts (vary by polity) | 2-3 | RING-2 PENDING |
| C9 | Time-bound status facts | 3 | attestation layer, never the chain |
| C10 | Dependent & cross-referential facts | 1-3 | derived_from seals the dependency graph |

Universally-quantified rules ("if x is prime and x > 2, x is odd") are out
of scope entirely (SCOPE S1): the ledger seals claims, not procedures.
Character inventories are C6 via Unicode code points (SCOPE S2
clarification). The intake gate cites the witnessed mandatory-conditions
ruleset for C4-class subjects.
