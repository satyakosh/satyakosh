# SCOPE.md — What Satyakosh refuses, and why

**Status: RECONSTRUCTED. The seven rules S1-S7 existed in the lost
original; this restatement is rebuilt from the locked decision record and
must be re-reviewed by the founder before this file is frozen and hashed
for genesis. See RECOVERY_NOTES.md.**

Satyakosh is a trust anchor, not an encyclopedia. It refuses whole
categories of content on purpose. A refusal at intake always cites one of
the rules below. These rules freeze at genesis; changing them later is a
public governance record.

- **S1 — Rules and algorithms.** The ledger seals claims, not procedures.
  "If x is prime and x > 2, x is odd" is a rule, not a fact record. Rule
  texts, if ever needed, enter as hashed documents.
- **S2 — Lexical facts.** Word meanings and translations are out of scope.
  A trust anchor that tries to be a dictionary anchors nothing.
  Clarification: characters are in scope as entities via their Unicode
  code points (external-standard adoption, Ring 2); words are not.
- **S3 — Fiction and works of imagination.** Claims about invented worlds
  have no external referent to verify.
- **S4 — News and current status.** Time-bound "current" facts live in the
  unsealed, signed attestation layer, never the chain.
- **S5 — Legal registries.** Satyakosh does not mirror company registers,
  land records, or similar state registries.
- **S6 — Opinion, evaluation, and prediction.** No claim whose truth
  depends on judgment or the future.
- **S7 — Derivable content.** Anything computable from sealed data is a
  consumer query, never a primary record.
